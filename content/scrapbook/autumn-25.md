Good to learn that September-October-November all blurring into one is, in fact, a fate that continues even after you've left education. In these past 3 months I have: had a job, been sacked, done 2 courses, been in a play, started selling with patches a shop, stopped selling patches with a shop, done an awful lot of travelling, volunteered at a zine fair, at a fabric reuse shop, at a charity that provides careers advice, had many many Teams meetings... Oh, and I suppose the only relevant one to you, reader, [I finally shared an essay I've been working on since January](https://thepatchworkpress.com/nonfic/paint/). Awesome.  

## CHAPTER ONE: September

I don't think it's fair to say too much about the job I had in September yet, but be assured once enough time has passed I will write an autobiographical piece about how truly insane my September job was. I intended to quit on the Friday night... and they sacked me on the Friday morning. How dare they take that from me! I was fuming. From unemployment I came, to unemployment I soon returned... So this took up most of my September, unfortunately, and very little creating art.

However, I did have the really exciting opportunity to volunteer at [Bradford Zine Fair](https://itsoninbradford.co.uk/events/bradford-zine-fair/)!
