These past few months I have learnt that no, September-October-November all blurring into one is _not_ a full-time education exclusive experience. In Autumn, I: had a job, quit the job, did 2 courses, was in a play, [made a Ko-fi](https://ko-fi.com/thepatchworkpress), did a _lot_ of travelling, volunteered at a zine fair, at a fabric reuse shop, had many many Teams meetings... Oh, and I suppose the only relevant one to you, reader, [I finally shared an essay I've been working on since last January](https://thepatchworkpress.com/nonfic/paint/). Awesome.  

## September

I am afraid I had a full-time job, briefly, in September. But don't worry, dear reader: from unemployment I came, and to unemployment I soon returned. Work took up most of my September, and there was very little creating art.

However, I did have the really exciting opportunity to volunteer at [Bradford Zine Fair](https://www.instagram.com/bradfordzinefair/)! It was a lot of fun; I talked to lots of awesome zinemakers and expanded my zine collection.

## October and November, which were actually of note

### Paint (an essay)

I was so pleased that I finally finished writing [Paint: How To Reclaim Your Body Under Capitalism](https://thepatchworkpress.com/nonfic/paint/), an essay I've been working on for nearly a year! If you haven't read it, Paint is a creative non-fiction essay exploring my relationship to make-up and my identity as a non-binary trans man. I painted (haha) an original piece to go with it here on The Patchwork Press, which I was really pleased with:

I'm hoping to eventually make Paint into an illustrated chapbook, featuring this illustration and maybe with a few new ones, too. For now, you can read it [here](https://thepatchworkpress.com/nonfic/paint/); I hope you enjoy!

### Bits & Bobs

I was also in a play! I helped with the arts group 6million+ to create _Freedom Desert_ exploring the East African campaign in WW2, which we performed as part of the [Our Freedom event at the Lawrence Batley Theatre](https://www.thelbt.org/blog/a-look-back-at-our-freedom-commemorating-and-celebrating-together/) in Huddersfield. This was my first time doing anything theatre since I was a kid, so it was a lot of fun to get back involved.

In the final week of October, I did the [Master the Game course by Proper Job](https://www.properjob.org.uk/master-the-game). I would absolutely recommend it if you run a creative small business - it really helped me to get my ideas in order and meet lots of people with similar interests! I did a second course with Proper Job in November, the [Creative Skills Bootcamp](https://www.properjob.org.uk/creative-skills-bootcamp), which was also a lot of fun. Both are completely free!

### Cleckheaton: Eighth Wonder of the Spen Valley?

After the last eighteen months of admiring other people's zines with wistful longing, I finally, FINALLY, finished one of my own. Inspired by the fact that I frequently tell people (who live locally, I might add) where I'm from and they respond, "Where?" I thought the best way to explain where I'm from might be through a zine. So, I present to you, Cleckheaton.

I hope that finally finishing a zine is a bit like unclogging a drain. After trying to sit down and make a zine for many months, I'm really pleased I actually did it, and I hope this means I can actually make more of the zines and chapbooks I have ideas for. 

Reader, I invite you to do the same. Have you had an idea for a zine, or a craft, or a piece of art, floating around your head for a while but you've been putting off making it for whatever reason? This is your call to sit down and do it. Starting is always the hardest part.

I leave this message with you as we enter the depths of winter. I hope you have a restful December, or a productive December, whichever of the two you need the most right now!

And I hope _I_ finally finish that book review I've been trying to write since the end of August.
