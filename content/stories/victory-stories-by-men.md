I always preferred the name my mother gave you, Boadicea;  
it moved smoothly over my northern tongue, more regal than  
your guttural cries for Victory. I suppose those  
nineteenth-century imperialists liked you that way,   
clean sounds, soft edges, a far cry from  
the barbaric language you died to defend:  
Rule Britannia, Boudicca, they jeered,  
if only you could stand so tall.  
Fierce woman painted in smoke,  
is the name of your battle buried  
in ash under London?  
Only the tale of  
your glorious martyrdom  
has the power to obscure the voice of  
the Englishman who rewrote  
your victory  
as his own.
